I only drive Bugattis

16 October 2013

What a difference some sleep makes. I’m finally caught up on rest after the weekend. It’s Wednesday. It’s not even about having crazy weekends, it’s about not working yourself to death(you’ll always feel like you don’t have enough time if you simply feel that way), then not blowing off steam recklessly (sleeping at 5am two nights in a row will really rock your sleep schedule). It’s about balance. Knowing how to take time to pace and not work 14 days in a row (bad idea), then knowing how to go see friends but not stay out late to make up for working 14 days straight (bad idea + bad idea = just bad idea).

I’ve been hearing this from various sources lately, and it’s a good piece to keep around – “It’s a marathon, not a sprint.” It’s going to take time for new concepts, a brand new way of thinking, a brand new way of looking at the world, to truly sink in. This is probably gonna change the way your brain puts everything together, so be patient. If you do find yourself in a mad scram up a 90deg slope, just know, this is kinda what it is supposed to be.

I’m progressing. I’m soaking it all up. It’s coming. It’s been a month really. I’m relearning America too (personal struggle). Show up, show up, show up. Show up, pace, keep fun, keep joy, and cruise. Eye-of-the-storm. Eezy peezy.

Today, we continued chopping up and rolling out like Navy Seals on this kickstarter project. We scrum in Trello, then break, handle our parts, then git push, clear our commits, then group pull back down like we are sitting at some cotton spider-loom of interweb construction. With guidance, we managed to install Stripe’s credit-card payment API onto our page as checkout. It’s simple and barebones, but exciting. Mostly because to test it, I keep paying myself lots of imaginary ducats and my customer count keeps climbing. Soon I’ll be rolling deep like 37signals and DHH, getting 10,000+ github commits, people flying me to talk about which Ruby gem is my favorite, which unicode symbol I prefer on my prompt during the Autumn Equinox, hipchatting honey-babies in my Bugatti, as I drip start-up ideas all over the place like a child discovering honey, money.

Man, it’s a different world.

vicissitudes

15 October 2013

Day 2 of kickstarter/crowdtilt. We break chunks. We scrum with the best of them. We are the scrummiest. Sometimes you non-stop win, other times you tear your sweater on a splinter – it’s all good, you’re still in the game. Today, tackled and cleared up (to a certain extent) more under-hood Rails voodoo. Associations are like untangling really thin and long earphone cables. Patience, man, patience. You wanna hear the music, don’t you? Did you know all about these things Rails is doing behind your back? Creating 11 methods just because you said ‘has_many’? Hey. Surprise on me.

It’s coming together, though.

Any progress is good progress. And a whole bunch of other mantra type shit. It’s all true.

Until it’s not true.

Our usual Fullstack Tuesday dinners was had yesterday when Andy, a freelance developer came by to share his vision of a trans-global life – free to work, free to be (which in his case is running marathons all over the world including Kenya and Alaska(?). His current client wouldn’t care if he was gone for 6mos on a beach in Thailand as long as his git pushes kept coming through and clean. I’m willing to stretch it to 1 or 2 yrs. First, got to get the builder skills up though, wrestle some open source, get a few gems named after me and my body parts.

Late in the day, I invented an app that will aggregate people’s grandmas and then farm them out to stranger’s homes where they cook people really kickass home-meals for money. Grandma Swap. Or, maybe more like Grandma Pimp, huh. Will the SPCA be mad at me? I promise they’re all free-range grandmas. If anyone is interested in trusting me with your grandmas, let me know. I’m an old lady’s dream.

pieces

08 October 2013

After a weekend, that didn’t feel much like a weekend because it was spent catching up on pre-work and learning about Rails, Monday rolled on forth with more Rails. I was kinda glad I stayed in and brushed up, and by brushed up, I mean learned for the first time, Rails, because we dove right into it today. Sinatra – out. Rails in. Rails is crazy cause with 2 command line returns, there’s this mass of folders and controllers, routes, models, html/css that’s just blegghgh right there. Kinda like how this morning I awoke to my friend’s cat wailing and making caulking-gun sounds. I open the door, and there it was. All this vomit everywhere. Hello, Ruby on Rails generate scaffold function!

Still working out the kinks, the connections, and my feelings towards this whole framework. I went to a childhood friend’s place for dinner and he asked me, so what does Rails do when you build a website? Good question. I just told him it’s like some sort of glue that seeps in and makes things easier between html and SQL, and everything… um, yeah. That’s Rails! Get’s all up in there, you know? Kinda like that cat vomit is all up inbetween the wood planks. That’s part of the house now.

Oh man, Rails, what have they been feeding you?

it’s hard, but we walk anyway

05 October 2013

I wish I had some great metaphor or anecdote that would move you right here, crumble the mountains and shake the shores, but I don’t. I just got reality in my pockets, and life in my veins. And, it’s gonna have to be enough.

It’s hard, but we walk anyway.

This is informed both by the current state of affairs in code-land, but also by my own personal affairs back home. The bridge isn’t hard to forge.

It’s hard, but we walk anyway.

Friday, we worked on crawlers to gank links off our Stackworld pages from the day before. Gank, recursive; self-replicating viruses of gankdom.

Saturday, I’m here again, but I’m not really here. I’m giving it all I got in time, energy, and mind, but other things got my heart. And, I think that’s okay. Not everything’s a straight-line, even in immersive 3month programs. Goals, achievements, careers, fancy/expensive pieces of paper, jobs, all these little applications, still need to be made available by the server of health, love, and life. I’m especially reminded of that today.

It’s hard, but we walk anyway.
