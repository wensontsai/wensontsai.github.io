Day #1

17 September 2013

The morning was spent shaking off nerves with laughs, dark jokes, and dancing as we threw ourselves in a course of improv. It was a great way to get out of our shells, look dumb in front of a room of strangers/new friends, and get us in the right mindset to be team-players.

Lessons to take away:

  1. Yes, AND… # always accept what your fellow player gives you, AND try to give them something back that they can work with, a little gift. “No”/questions take the energy somewhere else unproductive with the flow

  2. Always show up # no matter if you are scared, unprepared or what, just show up and create in the moment from there. That’s the most important.

  3. Help them out # when you see a fellow player in trouble, jump in and save them. Don’t let them die alone out there!

The afternoon was just as invigorating as we jumped straight into setting up our Fullstack profile pages off a minimal template. It was HTML/CSS in action, as well as a challenge in vision – to create a stylesheet, requires understanding how to work within the defaults/habits of HTML/CSS. It was a leap into madness with divs, nested divs, floats, and the beauty hack of the “clearfix.” This was all after setting up Github and learning how to commits as well.

I got most of the page looking like it was supposed to, but then spent the last 2 hours trying to get some links to hug right and to the bottom. It just wasn’t meant to be. As a last ditch effort, I tried to renest one of the divs by putting the main quote into a class of its own, and leaving the links outside to text-align: right. THEN, I noticed a div tag that I forgot to close. I closed that, then scooted a few things around, and the page just went to shit. Everything became unaligned, and CTRL-Z became the very bestest friend I could have ever had. Backsteps off the lava cliff jump. And so, it wasn’t perfect, but it was something.

I rushed out fast for the first time in a week – headed off to the Apple Store to replace the battery on my brand new Macbook Air. Here’s to 12 hours of battery time.
