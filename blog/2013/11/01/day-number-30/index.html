come together

01 November 2013

I am excited that we are nearing the end of the stack. There’s lots of things I would love to have time to go back and work on. I feel like I got the “Europe in 3 days!” tour. I wanna go back, get lost on some old cobbled backstreets, chill out for an hour at a cafe, and really understand why French people get so uppity when you speak to them in English.

I am more and more convinced that hacking is definitely a DO-type endeavor. Tomorrow I will begin to work on implementing a site that can link up various video screens, so people can play music together, attend a concert, a talk, or other social event. I’m werking to twerk the angles to see how it can be more of a social experiment – a platform free for the people to use and experiment with. Teach me a thing or three, please. I am imagining like your own Ticketmaster, your own Madison Square Garden. A virtual concert hall/theatre/lecture space for selling tickets to whatever you can, whatever your followers love for you to share. First things first though, just to get 3 screens to show up, and be able to throw that into a room. Tiny step. Long journey.

Front-end vs. back-end: Front-end is exciting. Back-end is necessary – it’s solid, got fortitude, but no one notices the trunk of trees (unless there’s a problem). People drive upstate to watch leaves change though. I am interested in getting more into UI/UX.
