innit

15 November 2013

I’ve been away. I’ll tell you why. I was feeling crushed by the crush of the self-crush. Learning the full stack had become the beast that I could never feed enough – not enough energy, study, effort, time, weekends, or nights – the beast had become a burden. Advice of the head is hard to seep in the heart somehow, the membrane is pro- or anti- lipids I can’t remember which one. I realized the importance of not grinding your wheels and rails to a pulp – even choo-choos have limits – yet, there I was gunning like some 16-yr old rodeo bullrider with everything to prove and nothing to lose, all burn all the time.

I got 4 days away – I was determined to work during those 4 days, but as soon as I entered back into the lazy obese air of my place of birth, sitting in the oppressive quiet of my mother’s house, backbone.js was gone. Backbone.js couldn’t be further away – iterm and sublime had no place in my plane of reality anymore. It was just a few days, but I feel more grounded and grasping of a perspective on everything that is going on again. Paradigm shifts are a must. Rest is a must.

Here are some bullet points organized in no particular theme, purpose, nor order:

  • about 2 weeks ago, Emily from MongoDB came to speak to us, and for the first time in my life I felt aroused about a database

  • my endurance has increased – I can now google search about something I know nothing about over and over for longer periods of time with less cop-out

  • I work better with earplugs in and music on

  • New York Times Hackathon this weekend !

  • I like capstone-project phase and the analog Trello system we got going in the classroom on a plane of glass. Lewis and Clark up in the digital sphere with only the occasional courier dispatch.

  • Going home at 9pm no matter if I’m feeling energetic or not is a life-saver must law

  • It’s all gonna get done – it just will. No matter how many hours you put in or kill/sacrifice yourself for the work – it’ll get done somehow. Often in the end, it doesn’t even matter. A friend wiser than me who’s lived through it told me.

  • Coding remains a subclass that inherits from the class of life. Never forget it.

  • Charlotte yoga is a lifesaver. Can she do Rails tutorials in her soothing voice? Breathe in through the nostrils, Movies has_many Actors. Now breathe out through the mouth – AHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHhhh h h

  • 4 weeks left. Let’s have some fucking fun.
