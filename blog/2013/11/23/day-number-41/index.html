T minus and it's on

23 November 2013

There is literally 2 weeks left of Fullstack Academy. Literally.

Thinking about it makes me bittersweet. Even though a ton has happened, in a short amount of time – yet at the same time, it feels like it’s been such a long time coming, a long road – man, we were just getting started, and now we gotta leave the nest.

Two weeks.

  • Recruitment activities commence now
  • Start new demo project to sharpen Backbone.js skills and get heavy on the backend
  • Wrap up pretty the Escapergram project (my idea of an alternate universe escape for picture-lovers through the day, where at any time you could take a mini-vacation and see what was going on in another part of the world. It pulls feed from the Instagram API, mapping from the Google maps API, and uses d3.js on the landing page).

Yesterday, while doing our Friday demos, someone had the brilliant idea to live-post pictures of me demo-ing into the feed at the Fullstack latitude and longitude (within 5000 meter radius). It’d been a slow-brain day – I was just talking through the work I had added, but this shook me awake. Interacting with the crowd is a must – that’s where the fun is.

Waiting and manually refreshing the page, some photos might a slipped through (sadness). But as a result, now Escapergrams got AJAX auto-refresh (every 10 secs). Escape into the now to your heart’s content, people!
